It's always better to prototype small models first

The best way to start prototyping is always spend some time thinking carefully when you design your model in the computer. In case that you started with a 2d model is a good behavior to make the 3d model from your plans and test if everything fits together in the virtual world. If you use standarized wood for your design you can easily scale it down 1/3 or 1/4 to cut your files in the lasercutter using thinner wood or cardboard so you can see the real shape of your design and make some changes or check that everything works fine.

Even if you take lot of attention you can break sometimes mills. Be aware with the cnc is a very powerfull machine and it easy to make expensives mistakes. Before starting to cut double or triple check everything and do an "air cut" of you files to check if the machine is moving correctly or not.

V carve 3d preview

The camm program most students use is vcarve shopbot edition.It has key feature,3d carving preview, very usefull to the precked your toolpaths and movements on the machine so you can precheck how you machine it is going to be cutted and possible errors/mistakes.

ALWAYS PLAN WHERE YOU PUT YOUR SCREWS TO HOLD YOUR MATERIAL!!! OR YOU WILL HIT ONE AND BREAK THE MILL

Be safe and only aproach the machine working space when it is completely stopped and the spindle is off.
